Tuna Casserole

Tuna Casserole is a surprisingly tasty dish that young people seem to fear. Sure the name IS unflattering, and its not a totally common household dish, but this dish deserves a chance. Hearty, healthy, and good for the soul, a good casserole is just what we need in trying times like these. So delay no further and heat up that oven, the casserole is waiting.

Ingredients

Serves 8-10 people

  • 2 cups uncooked macaroni
  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 2 (6oz) cans tuna in vegetable oil, drained
  • onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 stalks of celery, chopped (survey your crowd for this one, celery is polarizing)
  • 1 (10 3/4 ounce) can cream of mushroom soup, and or (10 3/4 ounce) can chicken soup
  • 1/2 cup of milk
  • 5 tablespoons of butter, melted
  • 2 cups breadcrumbs

Preparation

  • Cook macaroni until el dente' and drain.
  • Sautee the onions and celery first if you don't want a crunchy casserole
  • Combine all ingredients.
  • Pour into baking dish.
  • Mix topping (bread crumbs and butter) and put on casserole.
  • Bake at 350 degrees for 30 minutes.
