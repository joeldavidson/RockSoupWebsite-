Lemon Chicken with Rice

Chicken breast and rice is workday classic. However, it can be a little bland. If you want to add some flavor and intrigue to your weekday chicken, then try this recipe. With the addition of a just a few ingreadients, you can turn a boring old chicken breast into a delicous dinner. This recipe is courtesy of Oliver Tufte photo courtesy of thechunkychef.com.

Ingredients

Serves 3-4 people

  • 1 cup rice (long grain or brown)
  • 2 cups water
  • 4 chicken breasts
  • 1 lemon
  • 1-2 tablespoons olive oil (any vegtable oil will work)
  • 2 teaspoons salt (a couple pinches)
  • 2 teaspoons pepper
  • 2 teaspoons Italian seasoning if you have it or a couple pinches of Rosemary, Parsley, and Thyme
  • Fresh parsley to garnish if you'd like

Preparation

  • Bring 2 cups of water to a boil
  • While this is coming to a boil, preheat your oven to 350°F
  • Place chicken breasts on a plate or cutting board and coat with olive oil
  • Now rub the chicken down with the salt, pepper, and italian seasoning
  • Cut the lemon into 6 even slices
  • Squeeze three of the slices over the lemons and rub in, set the other 3 aside
  • Place the chicken into a lighlty oiled baking tray along with all 6 lemon slices, squeezed and unsqueezed
  • Once the water is boiling add 1 cup rice of your choosing to the water
  • Once the oven is pre-heated add the baking tray with lemon and chicken (cooks for 20-25 minutes)
  • The rice should also take about 20-25 mins to cook.
  • After you pull the chicken out, give it 5 mins to cool, then plate the chicken and rice together and squeeze the cooked lemon slices over top. The cooked lemon juice provides a delicous, sweet and tangy sauce.
  • Add the fresh parsley and enjoy.
